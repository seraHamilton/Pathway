What does it mean to be an auditory learner?

Auditory learners are people who learn best through hearing and speaking information, like song lyrics, podcasts, and lectures. They also tend to better understand things that are explained to them verbally, and enjoy sharing their own information verbally. People with an auditory learning style often utilize the tones of people's voices to pull more information than what is directly stated, inferring subtle motivations and meanings behind words.

Advantages

  • Most forms of teaching and employment require some type of auditory based skill, whether listening or speaking. Being an auditory learner can be helpful in presentations, since explanations and thoughts translate well to speaking.
  • Many people claim that being an auditory learner makes it easier to mulitask, because they have the ability to interpret auditory cues and apply them to associated tasks.

Disadvantages

  • Auditory learners can sometimes find it difficult to concentrate in environments with a high degree of background noise, or even smaller distractions.
  • Conversely, auditory learners might easily find themselves unable to complete work in complete silence, devoid of the stimuli they learn best from.
