Kinesthetic Learning

What does it mean to be a kinesthetic learner?

Kinesthetic learners are people who learn best through interactive approaches or 3D models to visualize concepts. These people tend to enjoy math, science, and engineering as these subjects frequently involve hands-on projects.

Advantages

  • Many kinesthetic learners find that they are able to quikly understand the bigger picture of a concept when being taught, studying, etc.
  • Kinesthetic learners can also have an easier time commiting information to their long term memory, as they connect ideas, etc. to what they have physically done instead of trying to internalixe information from presentations, etc. without aid.

Disadvantages

  • Kinesthetic learners may find that it is hard to concentrate when not engaging in the movement that they are used to utilizing while learning.
  • Classes often invole large readings and long lectures, directly conflicting with the kinesthetic learning style. Because of this, kinesthetic learners may find that it is difficult to concentrate and absorb information in these environments.
