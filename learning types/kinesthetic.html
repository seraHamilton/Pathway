Kinesthetic Learning

What does it mean to be a kinesthetic learner?

Kinesthetic learners are people who learn best through interactive approaches or 3D models to visualize concepts. These people tend to enjoy math, science, and engineering as these subjects frequently involve hands-on projects.

Advantages

  • Most forms of teaching and employment require some type of auditory based skill, whether listening or speaking. Being an auditory learner can be helpful in presentations, since explanations and thoughts translate well to speaking.

Disadvantages

  • Auditory learners can sometimes find it difficult to concentrate in environments with a high degree of background noise, or even smaller distractions.
  • Conversely, auditory learners might easily find themselves unable to complete work in complete silence, devoid of the stimuli they learn best from.
