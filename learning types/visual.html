Visual Learning

What does it mean to be an visual learner?

Visual learners are people who learn and retain more effectively when information is presented visually rather than verbally. While the concept of "visual learning" is often associated with graphic design and the use of visuals, it is also used to refer to any means of presenting information to learners that is more effective than verbal presentations. This can be delivered through any combination of media, including multimedia, photographs, diagrams, charts, or graphs.

Disadvantages

  • Auditory learners can sometimes find it difficult to concentrate in environments with a high degree of background noise, or even smaller distractions.
  • Conversely, auditory learners might easily find themselves unable to complete work in complete silence, devoid of the stimuli they learn best from.
