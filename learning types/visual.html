Visual Learning

What does it mean to be an visual learner?

Visual learners are people who learn and retain more effectively when information is presented visually rather than verbally. While the concept of "visual learning" is often associated with graphic design and the use of pictures, it is also used to refer to any visual means of presenting information to learners that is more effective than verbal presentations. This can be delivered through any combination of media, including multimedia, photographs, diagrams, charts, or graphs.

Advantages

  • Oftentimes, graphics and images are used in presentations and projects to communicate information in a convenient manner. This frequency of visual aids allows visual learners to struggle less in learning in the context of their learning type.
  • A wide variety of studies have shown that visual information is processed so it can be better recalled, allowing ease of memorization.

Disadvantages

  • Visual learners may have difficulty understanding more difficult topics as more abstract concepts become harder to imagine.
  • Additionally, these people may struggle wtih verbal directions or have trouble learning in lectures.
